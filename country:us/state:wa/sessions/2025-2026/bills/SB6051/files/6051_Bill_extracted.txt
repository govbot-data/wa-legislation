It is important that schools are provided flexibility to determine how best to meet the needs of their students and work with their local communities. It is the intent of the legislature to provide schools increased flexibility by authorizing school districts to grant waivers or partial waivers of state laws and rules for schools located within the school district. Additionally, the legislature intends to specifically authorize school districts to use any savings resulting from not having to implement a law or rule that was waived or partially waived. However, for each waiver granted by the school district that results in a savings, a portion of the savings must be awarded to each school that requested the waiver and to any teacher at a school that brought the idea to the principal to request the waiver.NEW SECTION.Â Â Sec. 2. A new section is added to chapter 28A.320 RCW to read as follows:(1) As specifically provided in chapters in this title, the board of directors of each school district may grant waivers, or partial waivers, of state laws and rules to individual schools within the district. The school board shall grant waivers in accordance with this section.(2) To apply for a waiver, a school principal must submit an application to the school district board of directors that identifies the laws and rules requested to be waived and the rationale for the request. The rationale must identify how granting the waiver will improve student learning or the delivery of education services in the school.(3) The school district board of directors must provide time for public review and comment regarding any waiver request at a regular meeting of the school district board of directors.(4) The duration, renewal, and rescission of the waiver must be determined by the school district board of directors.(5) The following may not be waived:(a) Compliance with local, state, and federal health, safety, parents' rights, civil rights, and nondiscrimination laws;(b) Provision of a program of basic education, that meets the goals in RCW 28A.150.210;(c) Employment of certificated instructional staff as required in RCW 28A.410.025. However, school districts may hire noncertificated instructional staff of unusual competence and in exceptional cases as authorized in RCW 28A.150.203(7);(d) Compliance with the employee record check requirements in RCW 28A.400.303;(e) Adherence to generally accepted accounting principles and be subject to financial examinations and audits as determined by the state auditor, including annual audits for legal and fiscal compliance;(f) Compliance with the annual performance report under RCW 28A.655.110;(g) Compliance with the open public meetings act in chapter 42.30 RCW and the public records act in chapter 42.56 RCW; and(h) Compliance with state and federal constitutional provisions.(6) A school district may not include provisions in a collective bargaining agreement that limit the school district's authority to grant waivers under this section.(7) Each school district board of directors granting waivers of state laws and rules shall submit to the superintendent of public instruction and the state board of education a list of laws and rules waived by the school district board of directors in accordance with this section.(8) School districts are authorized to use any savings resulting from not having to implement a state law or rule waived by the school district board of directors. However, each school district must award a portion of the savings to the first school that requested the waiver that resulted in a savings and to the first teacher at a school who brought the idea to the principal to request the waiver. No award may be granted in excess of $10,000 or 10 percent of the actual net savings, whichever amount is less. No award may be granted to a school in excess of 10 percent of the actual net savings, and no amount may be granted to a teacher in excess of $10,000.NEW SECTION.Â Â Sec. 3.
