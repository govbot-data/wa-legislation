ï»¿SENATE RESOLUTION8664BySenator NoblesWHEREAS, The Washington state sports community is celebrating prominence on the world stage as a site for the 2026 FIFA World Cup; andWHEREAS, More than half of youth in Washington state participate in organized athletics; andWHEREAS, Organized youth sports and physical activity can provide essential infrastructure for youth well-being with lifelong benefits; andWHEREAS, High quality youth sports support academic engagement and school attendance, promote resiliency and physical health, and protect against anxiety and depression; andWHEREAS, Only 24 percent of youth statewide meet the recommended 60 minutes of daily physical activity, with participation rates declining as children age; andWHEREAS, Washington state ranks 38th in youth well-being, according to Mental Health America; andWHEREAS, Disparities exist in access to organized sports, especially for girls, youth of color, children with disabilities, immigrants, LGBTQ+ youth, and children from low-income families, with youth who don't speak English at home nearly three times more likely to never participate in sports; andWHEREAS, Young people thrive when their coaches are trained in youth development principles and when they have access to trained professionals who can assess injuries common to young athletes like concussion, heat stroke, stress fractures, knee injuries, and ankle sprains; andWHEREAS, Despite significant progress in concussion education through the Zackery Lystedt Law, there is a critical need for broader awareness and systemic training in order to prevent abuse and injuries; andWHEREAS, Early specialization in youth athletics has been found to cause overuse injuries and burnout; andWHEREAS, Only approximately half of coaches have reported feeling prepared to support athletes with mental health concerns; andWHEREAS, Many communities lack sufficient fields and facilities to accommodate sports programming and public and private investment is needed to ensure more young people have access to spaces to play; andWHEREAS, Less traditional emerging sports such as ultimate frisbee, pickleball, flag football, roller derby, mountain biking, parkour, lacrosse, and martial arts provide valuable opportunities for youth development and should be promoted alongside mainstream sports; andWHEREAS, Adaptive sports are an opportunity for youth with disabilities to participate in and experience sports; andWHEREAS, All youth should be able to enjoy sports as a joyful and social experience;NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, That the state of Washington recognizes the transformative benefits of youth sports opportunities; andBE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That the state promotes inclusive, affordable, and high quality sports programs and seeks to address disparities in access to organized sports, particularly for marginalized youth, to ensure widespread participation statewide; andBE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That the state recognizes the value of trained coaches and athletic staff to ensure all young athletes are safe and feel welcomed and supported and supports injury prevention and safety education for adults involved in youth athletics; andBE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That the state promotes nontraditional emerging sports as opportunities for more youth to be involved in athletics; andBE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That the Washington state Senate celebrate youth sports initiatives as a means of supporting the well-being of youth in Washington state.
