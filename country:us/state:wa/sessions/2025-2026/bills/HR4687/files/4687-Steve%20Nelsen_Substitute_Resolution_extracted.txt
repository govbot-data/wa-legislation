ï»¿HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 2026-4687HR 4687ByRepresentatives Bergquist, Bronoske, Griffey, Burnett, Paul, and OrmsbyWHEREAS, Steve Nelsen has devoted 34 years of exemplary public service to the state of Washington, including 22 years of distinguished service to the Law Enforcement Officers' and Firefighters' (LEOFF) Plan 2 Retirement Board; andWHEREAS, Mr. Nelsen served as the first-ever Executive Director of the Law Enforcement Officers' and Firefighters' Plan 2 Retirement Board and played a central role in creating, building, and successfully operating the agency from its inception; andWHEREAS, Throughout his tenure, Mr. Nelsen provided steady, knowledgeable, and principled leadership, earning the respect and trust of board members, legislators, stakeholders, and colleagues across state government; andWHEREAS, Working in close partnership with the Board, Mr. Nelsen helped strengthen the Law Enforcement Officers' and Firefighters' Plan 2 retirement system for its members and beneficiaries; andWHEREAS, Mr. Nelsen made substantial contributions toward long-term plan funding operations, helping to ensure the fiscal integrity, sustainability, and sound governance of the Law Enforcement Officers' and Firefighters' Plan 2 retirement system; andWHEREAS, Through his leadership and expertise, Mr. Nelsen has consistently demonstrated a deep understanding of retirement policy and a commitment to honoring the service and sacrifice of Washington's public safety professionals; andWHEREAS, Mr. Nelsen has been steadfastly committed to the mission of the agency and the Board, embodied in the guiding principle, "They keep us safe, we keep them secure," and has consistently centered the needs of law enforcement officers, firefighters, and their families in his work; andWHEREAS, Mr. Nelsen demonstrated a strong commitment to trustee education, ensuring board members were well informed and equipped to fulfill their fiduciary responsibilities through ongoing education, collaboration, and access to expert resources; andWHEREAS, In recognition of his dedication and service to the public safety community, Mr. Nelsen has been honored as an honorary firefighter; andWHEREAS, Mr. Nelsen's career has been marked by integrity, institutional knowledge, collaboration, and an unwavering dedication to public service and the well-being of the members of the Law Enforcement Officers' and Firefighters' Plan 2 Retirement Board;NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, That the Washington State House of Representatives honor and commend Steve Nelsen for his outstanding service and lasting contributions to the State of Washington and to the Law Enforcement Officers' and Firefighters' Plan 2 retirement system; andBE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That the House extend its sincere appreciation and best wishes to Mr. Nelsen for a fulfilling and rewarding retirement; andBE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That copies of this resolution be transmitted to Steve Nelsen and to the Law Enforcement Officers' and Fire Fighters' Plan 2 Retirement Board.I hereby certify this to be a true and correct copy ofResolution 4687 adopted by the House of RepresentativesFebruary 24, 2026__________________________Bernard Dean, Chief Clerk
