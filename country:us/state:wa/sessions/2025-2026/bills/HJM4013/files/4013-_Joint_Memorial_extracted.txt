ï»¿H-3089.1HOUSE JOINT MEMORIAL 4013State of Washington69th Legislature2026 Regular SessionByRepresentatives Keaton and WalshTO THE HONORABLE BOB FERGUSON, GOVERNOR OF THE STATE OF WASHINGTON, AND TO THE WASHINGTON STATE OFFICE OF FINANCIAL MANAGEMENT:We, your Memorialists, the Senate and House of Representatives of the State of Washington, in legislative session assembled, respectfully represent and petition as follows:WHEREAS, Public Law 119-21, enacted July 4, 2025, established a federal tax credit for contributions to scholarship granting organizations that provide educational assistance to eligible K-12 students; andWHEREAS, This federal investment program supports students in both public and private schools, ensuring that the majority of Washington's K-12 students attending public schools can access educational assistance for tutoring, special education services, extended day and enrichment programs, curriculum materials, technology, and other qualified expenses; andWHEREAS, This federal program enables individual taxpayers to direct up to $1,700 in federal tax credits annually toward educational assistance for students from working class and middle class families, transforming federal tax policy into direct educational support; andWHEREAS, Scholarships are restricted exclusively to students from working class and middle class families with household incomes at or below 300 percent of area median gross income, ensuring federal resources reach students who need educational support most; andWHEREAS, Based on Washington state's population and income demographics, this federal program could conservatively generate approximately $732 million in annual donations to scholarship granting organizations serving Washington students; andWHEREAS, The program becomes effective for taxable years ending after December 31, 2026, requiring timely state action to ensure Washington families can benefit beginning in 2027; andWHEREAS, Participation in this program is voluntary, requiring the governor to elect participation and identify qualifying scholarship granting organizations within the state; andWHEREAS, Opting into this federal program requires zero state expenditure, imposes no mandate on the state budget, creates no obligation for future state funding, and directs federal resources to Washington students that would otherwise go unused or benefit students in other states; andWHEREAS, Washington taxpayers will receive these federal tax credits whether Washington opts in or not, meaning the only question is whether their donations support educational assistance for Washington students or students in other participating states; andWHEREAS, Failure to opt in would result in Washington forfeiting an estimated $732 million annually in federal educational support that would instead flow to schools and students in states that have opted into the program, putting Washington students at a significant competitive and educational disadvantage; andWHEREAS, Scholarship granting organizations must meet stringent federal accountability standards, including annual financial audits, public reporting of expenditures, prohibition on self-dealing, income verification requirements, and mandatory spending of at least 90 percent of funds directly on student scholarships, ensuring federal dollars are used efficiently and reach eligible students; andWHEREAS, Washington state has a compelling interest in ensuring working families can access the same federal educational support available to families in other states, particularly when declining to participate would result in no benefit to Washington families while federal resources flow to students elsewhere;NOW, THEREFORE, Your Memorialists respectfully call on Governor Bob Ferguson to:(1) Exercise his authority under section 25F(g)(1)(B) of the internal revenue code to elect Washington state's participation in the Federal tax-credit scholarship program for K-12 educational expenses established by Public Law 119-21, and to do so without delay to ensure no Washington family is denied access to these federal benefits; and(2) Direct the appropriate state agency or designate an appropriate entity to establish a process for identifying and listing qualified scholarship granting organizations operating in Washington state, as required by section 25F(g) of the internal revenue code; and(3) Complete the election and submit the required list of scholarship granting organizations to the internal revenue service by January 1, 2027, or as early as practicable, to ensure Washington families can benefit from the program beginning in the 2027 tax year;BE IT RESOLVED, That the Legislature declares that opting into this federal program will:(1) Direct an estimated $732 million in annual federal education funding to Washington students at zero cost to state taxpayers;(2) Provide educational assistance to students from working class and middle class families attending both public and private schools throughout Washington;(3) Transform federal tax credits into direct scholarships for allowable education expenses, including tutoring, special education services, educational therapies, curriculum materials, technology, and other qualified K-12 expenses;(4) Ensure Washington families receive the same federal educational support as families in participating states, rather than allowing these federal resources to flow exclusively to students in other states that have opted into the program;(5) Create no financial obligation for the state budget while leveraging federal investment to expand educational opportunities for working families; and(6) Subject all participating scholarship organizations to rigorous federal accountability standards, including financial audits, public reporting, and requirements to spend at least 90 percent of funds on student scholarships; andBE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That copies of this Memorial be immediately transmitted, to the Honorable Bob Ferguson, Governor of the State of Washington, and to the Washington State Office of Financial Management.--- END ---
