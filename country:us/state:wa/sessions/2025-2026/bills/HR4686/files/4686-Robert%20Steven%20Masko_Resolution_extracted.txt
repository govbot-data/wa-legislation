ï»¿HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 2026-4686HR 4686ByRepresentatives Leavitt, Bronoske, and RichardsWHEREAS, Robert Steven Masko was born on October 28, 1954, in Tacoma, Washington, and was a lifelong resident of Pierce county who dedicated his life to service, family, and community; andWHEREAS, Robert Masko graduated from Wilson High School in 1973 and earned a degree in criminal justice from Washington State University in 1977, reflecting an early and enduring commitment to public service; andWHEREAS, Robert Masko devoted nearly four decades to law enforcement with the Pierce County Sheriff's Department, serving honorably for 37 years and retiring as undersheriff, where he was widely respected as a leader, mentor, and public servant known for integrity, compassion, and professionalism; andWHEREAS, Throughout his career, Robert Masko treated every person with dignity and worked tirelessly to protect and strengthen the communities he served; andWHEREAS, Beyond his professional service, Robert Masko gave generously of his time as a Fircrest City Councilmember and through leadership and volunteer roles, including Law Enforcement Youth Camp Director, Civil Service Commissioner, youth group leader, youth sports coach, and local credit union board member and chair; andWHEREAS, Following his retirement, Robert Masko continued his commitment to community service through work supporting a local law enforcement nonprofit, the Fircrest Civil Service Oral Board, the University Place Public Safety Advisory Commission, his homeowners association, and numerous other local efforts; andWHEREAS, Robert Masko worked diligently to secure new playground equipment at Masko Park, leaving a lasting legacy for children and families in the community; andWHEREAS, Robert Masko was a devoted husband, father, grandfather, brother, and friend whose warmth, humor, and generosity touched all who knew him; andWHEREAS, Robert Masko passed away on September 13, 2025, at the age of 70, surrounded by his loving family, leaving behind a legacy of service, leadership, and community devotion that will endure for generations;NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, That the House of Representatives of the state of Washington honor and recognize the life and service of Robert Steven Masko for his extraordinary contributions to public safety, civic leadership, and the Pierce county community; andBE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That the House of Representatives extend its deepest support, sympathy, and respect to his wife, Dawn Masko, his children, grandchildren, extended family, friends, colleagues, and all those whose lives were enriched by his service and friendship; andBE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That copies of this resolution be transmitted by the Chief Clerk of the House of Representatives to the family of Robert Steven Masko.I hereby certify this to be a true and correct copy ofResolution 4686 adopted by the House of RepresentativesFebruary 20, 2026__________________________Bernard Dean, Chief Clerk
