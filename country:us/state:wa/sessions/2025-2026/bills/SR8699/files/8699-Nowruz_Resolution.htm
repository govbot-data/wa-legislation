WHEREAS, Nowruz, meaning "new day," is an ancient celebration with roots tracing back more than three millennia to the cultures of the Iranian plateau and surrounding regions, marking the arrival of the spring equinox and the renewal of the natural world, which in 2026 occurs on March 20th at 7:46 a.m.; and
WHEREAS, The observance of Nowruz is shared by nearly 300 million people worldwide, transcending religious and national boundaries, and is celebrated by communities, including those in Iran, Iraq, Afghanistan, Tajikistan, Uzbekistan, Azerbaijan, and Turkmenistan, and among Kurdish, Hazara, and other cultural groups across the Middle East and Central Asia; and
WHEREAS, Nowruz symbolizes renewal, hope, and the transition from winter into spring, reflecting themes of rebirth, growth, and the enduring resilience of humanity and nature alike; and
WHEREAS, For many observers, particularly members of the Iranian diaspora and broader Middle Eastern and Central Asian communities in Washington state, Nowruz represents not only a cultural tradition but also an affirmation of dignity, freedom, justice, and the aspiration for peace and self-determination; and
WHEREAS, Washington state is home to vibrant Middle Eastern and Central Asian communities whose members have enriched the state through contributions to education, public service, entrepreneurship, health care, the arts, advocacy, and civic leadership; and
WHEREAS, Many individuals from these regions have come to the United States seeking safety, opportunity, and the ability to live freely and openly, strengthening the cultural fabric and shared democratic values of our state; and
WHEREAS, In times marked by global uncertainty, grief, and hardship, including the loss and suffering experienced by families and communities abroad, the spirit of Nowruz calls upon us to embrace compassion, unity, and mutual understanding; and
WHEREAS, The celebration of Nowruz offers an opportunity for all Washingtonians to reflect on shared values of generosity, renewal, community care, and optimism for the year ahead;
NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, That the Washington state Senate recognize and honor the celebration of Nowruz and its deep historical and cultural significance, extend its solidarity and support to all communities observing Nowruz in times of both celebration and challenge, and wish all those celebrating a joyful, peaceful, and prosperous new year.
