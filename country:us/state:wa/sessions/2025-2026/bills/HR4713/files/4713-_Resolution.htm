WHEREAS, John Michael Sattgast was born in Ontario, Oregon, to Herman and Dorothy Sattgast, the youngest child in a blended family with seven older siblings; and
WHEREAS, He moved to and grew up in the rural community of New Plymouth, Idaho, where his father was a dairy farmer; and
WHEREAS, John graduated from New Plymouth High School and attended Boise State University, where he studied communications and public relations and eventually became news director at the student radio station KBSU; and
WHEREAS, He continued his radio reporting career at KYET/KWBJ Radio in Ontario, Oregon, KIDO Radio in Boise, Idaho, and KPQ Radio in Wenatchee, Washington, where he met Representative Clyde Ballard; and
WHEREAS, John met Debbie Herman from Payette, Idaho, in December 1980. Their dates often ended with John rushing to a news story, with Debbie by his side as he went on the air remotely to report. Always the editor, he even sent her love letters back with copy edits. Yet, Debbie still married John in June 1982; and
WHEREAS, They went on to have three beautiful children â David, Jason, and Julie. The Sattgast family has called Shelton home since 1994; and
WHEREAS, In 1991, with Representative Clyde Ballard's assistance, John was hired as a public information officer for House Republican Caucus Media Services in the Washington State House of Representatives; and
WHEREAS, In 2011, he became broadcast coordinator for House Republican Caucus Communications. Since then, John has produced more than 4,000 radio stories and coordinated countless interviews with radio stations for House Republican state representatives; and
WHEREAS, John has served as a public information officer for 63 House Republican state representatives, including four House Republican leaders, and served while House Republicans were in the majority, minority, and 49-49 tie; and
WHEREAS, He enjoys his extended family of past and present coworkers and state lawmakers, and loves United States history, patriotism, and country, jazz, and rock music; and
WHEREAS, During his time in the Washington State House of Representatives, John has been active with the National Conference of State Legislatures (NCSL), serving three terms as a board member of the Legislative Information and Communications Staff. In 2023, he received NCSL's Legislative Staff Achievement Award. In 2025, John's service was profiled by the organization in a piece titled: He's Had a 'Front-Row Seat' to Washington State History; and
WHEREAS, John has not only had a front-row seat to Washington state history, but he has played an important role in sharing those stories with the public for 35 years;
NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, That the Washington State House of Representatives honor and celebrate John Michael Sattgast for his dedicated service to the House Republican Caucus and, indeed, to the people of the entire state of Washington.
