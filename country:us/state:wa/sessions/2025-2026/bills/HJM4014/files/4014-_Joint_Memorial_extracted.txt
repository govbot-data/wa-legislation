TRUMP, PRESIDENT OF THE UNITED STATES, AND TO THE PRESIDENT OF THE SENATE AND THE SPEAKER OF THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES, AND TO THE SENATE AND HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE UNITED STATES, IN CONGRESS ASSEMBLED, AND TO PAM BONDI, THE ATTORNEY GENERAL OF THE UNITED STATES, AND TO THE UNITED STATES DEPARTMENT OF JUSTICE:We, your Memorialists, the Senate and House of Representatives of the State of Washington, in legislative session assembled, respectfully represent and petition as follows:WHEREAS, Independent journalist Brandi Kruse has conducted extensive investigations revealing potential fraud in Washington state's child care subsidy programs, including admissions by the state auditor that hundreds of millions of dollars in payments are impossible to properly audit due to noncompliance with federal tracking requirements; andWHEREAS, Kruse's reporting has uncovered whistleblower evidence of self-dealing within the state's community reinvestment program, where taxpayer funds appear to have been used for personal enrichment by individuals connected to nonprofits administering the program; andWHEREAS, Kruse has highlighted cases where women seeking debt relief through minority-focused programs reported being misled and mistreated, further indicating systemic issues in fund distribution; andWHEREAS, The Center Square has reported on potential fraud in Washington state's taxpayer-subsidized child care centers, including instances where licensed facilities received substantial subsidies despite appearing inactive or nonoperational; andWHEREAS, The Center Square investigations have drawn parallels to federal raids in Minnesota over similar child care fraud schemes, spotlighting vulnerabilities in Washington's Department of Children, Youth, and Families (DCYF) subsidy system that may have cost taxpayers millions; andWHEREAS, The Center Square has documented democratic lawmakers criticizing journalistic probes into these allegations, while republican lawmakers call for independent audits to address unaccounted funds nearing $500,000,000; andWHEREAS, The Center Square has reported frustration from legitimate day care owners over alleged fraud within the Somali community, with DCYF claiming to investigate reports but downplaying the extent of issues; andWHEREAS, Representative Jim Walsh, as Washington State Republican Party Chairman, has been involved in a lawsuit challenging the attorney general's disparagement of efforts to preserve public records related to widespread taxpayer fraud, alleging state employees altered and deleted records in violation of the Public Records Act; andWHEREAS, Walsh and the Washington State Republican Party, along with journalist Jonathan Choe, filed an injunction in Thurston County Superior Court to prevent the state from deleting public records tied to subsidized child care centers amid fraud allegations; andWHEREAS, The Washington Attorney General's Office has supported efforts to protect and expand investigative subpoena powers, as evidenced by coleading a Supreme Court brief arguing against restrictions that could hinder probes into violations of state laws, including consumer protection and antitrust issues; andWHEREAS, Such expansions could include broader authority for investigators to issue subpoenas and potentially use undercover tactics, raising concerns about overreach in light of ongoing fraud allegations; andWHEREAS, Attorney General Nick Brown has publicly advised citizens questioning potential fraud in Somali-run day cares to report harassment via the state's Hate Crimes & Bias Incident Hotline, framing independent investigations as potentially unsafe or harassing behavior; andWHEREAS, Brown's statements have been criticized as threats against journalists, with warnings from federal officials that such actions could violate civil rights and expose him to scrutiny; andWHEREAS, Attorney General Brown has acknowledged allegations of fraud in taxpayer-funded programs but pushed back on their credibility, stating no evidence of criminal activity while declining to provide updates on investigations into nonprofit fund misuse; andWHEREAS, Brown's office seeks to maintain broad investigative powers over businesses and individuals, yet has refused to substantiate or pursue many fraud claims, prioritizing harassment reports over fraud probes; andWHEREAS, These actions may constitute a potential violation of the Attorney General's oath of office, as they appear to prioritize political considerations over faithful enforcement of laws against fraud and abuse of public trust, with no apparent investigation or oversight from the Washington Supreme Court; andWHEREAS, Attorney General Brown has been accused of inappropriate conduct, including a Washington State Bar Association investigation into his failure to disclose office contracts in a legal brief, which was dismissed only after media inquiries, raising questions about transparency and ethical lapses;NOW, THEREFORE, Your Memorialists respectfully pray that the President of the United States direct the United States Department of Justice to initiate a thorough investigation into these allegations of fraud in Washington state's taxpayer-funded programs, the Attorney General's handling of such claims, potential violations of public records laws, and any related misconduct that may undermine public trust and federal oversight requirements.BE IT RESOLVED, That copies of this Memorial be immediately transmitted by the Secretary of State to the Honorable Donald J. Trump, President of the United States, Pam Bondi, the Attorney General of the United States, the United States Department of Justice, the President of the United States Senate, the Speaker of the House of Representatives, and each member of Congress from the State of Washington.--- END ---
