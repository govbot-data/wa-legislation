Wilson, and HolyRead first time 02/12/26.Referred to Committee on State Government, Tribal Affairs & Elections.BE IT RESOLVED, BY THE SENATE AND HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE STATE OF WASHINGTON, IN LEGISLATIVE SESSION ASSEMBLED:THAT, At the next general election to be held in this state the secretary of state shall submit to the qualified voters of the state for their approval and ratification, or rejection, an amendment to Article II, section 7 of the Constitution of the state of Washington to read as follows:Article II, section 7. ((No person shall be eligible to the legislature who shall not be a citizen of the United States and a qualified voter in the district for which he is chosen.))(1) A person filing for candidacy for the legislature, or seeking appointment in case of a vacancy, is eligible for election, appointment, holding office, and remaining in office only if that person:(a) Is a citizen of the United States of America;(b) Is a qualified voter in the district for which he or she is chosen;(c) Is at least 25 years old;(d) Has obtained a high school diploma or high school equivalency certificate;(e) Has not been convicted under the laws of this state, another state, the United States, or the substantial equivalent under foreign law, of a felony;(f) Has not been convicted under the laws of this state, another state, the United States, or the substantial equivalent under foreign law, of a gross misdemeanor;(g) Has not engaged in and does not engage in conduct requiring denial or revocation of certification as a peace officer or correctional officer, debarment from legal practice, or other denial or revocation of a professional license or certification;(h) Has received at least a general discharge under honorable conditions from any branch of the armed services for any military service if the person was in the military service;(i) Has never had a professional certification denied, revoked, or voluntarily surrendered, and not reinstated; and(j) Has completed a background investigation as provided in subsection (2) of this section.(2) Any person seeking election, or appointment in the case of a vacancy, to the legislature, must submit to a background investigation conducted by the state patrol to determine the person's suitability for office. The background investigation must have been completed within the prior six months, and include:(a) A check of criminal history;(b) Inquiry into whether the person has any past or present affiliations with extremist organizations;(c) A review of the person's social media accounts;(d) Verification of citizenship status;(e) A psychological examination; and(f) A polygraph or similar assessment.BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That the secretary of state shall cause notice of this constitutional amendment to be published at least four times during the four weeks next preceding the election in every legal newspaper in the state.--- END ---
