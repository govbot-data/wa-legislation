HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 2026-4679
HR 4679
ByRepresentatives Manjarrez, Pollet, Taylor, Chase, Dufault, Schmick, Mendoza, and Corry
WHEREAS, The Washington State Legislature recognizes the sovereignty, culture, and enduring heritage of the Yakama Nation and the importance of preserving Indigenous languages as an essential component of identity, history, and community; and
WHEREAS, The Confederated Tribes and Bands of the Yakama Nation has over 10,000 enrolled tribal members, representing one of the largest Native American nations in the state of Washington; and
WHEREAS, Ichishkiin, a Sahaptin language of the Yakama Nation, has been taught in Wapato public schools since 2011, beginning under the leadership of TuÌulhÉ¨nch (Roger Jacob) with the support and advocacy of Native American parents participating in the federal Johnson-O'Malley program; and
WHEREAS, The program currently serves students in grades four through 12. Students in grades six through 12 may enroll in Ichishkiin language courses as part of their academic pathway, and upper-level courses offer students the opportunity to earn college credit through Heritage University with three continuous years of study; and
WHEREAS, Wapato School District is the first high school in the state of Washington to enable students to earn the Washington State Seal of Biliteracy in an Indigenous language; and
WHEREAS, The excellence of the Ichishkiin language program has been recognized nationally, with the Wapato School District's Johnson-O'Malley program named Exemplary Program of the Year for Region 6 in 2025 by the National Johnson-O'Malley Association, and TuÌulhÉ¨nch (Roger Jacob) recognized as Region 6 Teacher of the Year for his outstanding leadership in Indigenous language education; and
WHEREAS, The Ichishkiin language program at Wapato School District serves as a model for language revitalization, academic achievement, and collaboration between public schools, tribal communities, and institutions of higher education;
NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, That the House of Representatives commend Wapato School District for its leadership in teaching the Ichishkiin language and for its commitment to preserving and advancing Indigenous languages through public education; and
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That the House of Representatives recognize the students, educators, families, and Yakama Nation community members whose dedication has made this program a success.
I hereby certify this to be a true and correct copy of
Resolution 4679 adopted by the House of Representatives
