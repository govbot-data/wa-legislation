ï»¿SENATE RESOLUTION8675BySenator SalomonWHEREAS, The people of Washington state and the people of Ukraine share a deep commitment to democracy, human rights, self-determination, and the rule of law; andWHEREAS, Washington state is home to more than 110,000 people of Ukrainian heritage and one of the largest populations of Ukrainian immigrants among the 50 states; andWHEREAS, Many Ukrainians immigrated to Washington state fleeing Soviet persecution for their political, cultural, and religious beliefs and seeking freedom and opportunity in the United States; andWHEREAS, Since February 2022, Washington state has welcomed close to 30,000 displaced Ukrainians seeking refuge from the Russian war and continues to be a welcoming and supportive home for those fleeing violence and persecution; andWHEREAS, Ukrainians and Ukrainian Americans in Washington state have enriched our communities through their leadership and contributions in agriculture, business, academia, government, health care, and the arts; andWHEREAS, On February 24, 2022, Russia launched a full-scale and unprovoked aggression against Ukraine, escalating a war that began in 2014, in the largest attack on a European nation since World War II; andWHEREAS, Russia's actions have resulted in widespread civilian casualties, mass displacement, destruction of critical infrastructure, and many reports of war crimes and crimes against humanity; andWHEREAS, The people of Ukraine are courageously defending their independence, democratic institutions, cultural identity, and right to choose their own future as a sovereign nation;NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, That the Washington State Senate express its deepest sympathy and solidarity with the people of Ukraine and with Ukrainian Americans, especially those with loved ones in harm's way; andBE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That the Washington State Senate commend the courage, resilience, and determination of the Ukrainian people and their armed forces in defending their nation and democratic future; andBE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That copies of this resolution be transmitted to the President of the United States, the Secretary of State, members of Washington state's congressional delegation, the Ambassador of Ukraine to the United States, and the Honorary Consulate of Ukraine in Seattle.
