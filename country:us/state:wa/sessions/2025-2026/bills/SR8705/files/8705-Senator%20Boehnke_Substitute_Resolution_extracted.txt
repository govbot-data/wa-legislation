WilsonWHEREAS, Senator Matt Boehnke has faithfully served the people of Washington state and the 8th Legislative District with dedication, integrity, and a steadfast commitment to public service; andWHEREAS, Raised in Kennewick and a lifelong resident of the Tri-Cities, Senator Boehnke first entered public service when he was elected to the Kennewick City Council in 2015, where he worked to strengthen local government, support public safety, and promote economic opportunity for his community; andWHEREAS, In 2018, Senator Boehnke was elected to the Washington State House of Representatives to represent the 8th Legislative District, which includes the city of Kennewick and parts of Richland, where he quickly established himself as a thoughtful advocate for innovation, workforce development, and policies that support families and local communities; andWHEREAS, In 2022, Senator Boehnke was elected to the Washington State Senate representing the 8th Legislative District and assumed office in January 2023, continuing his work on behalf of the communities of Kennewick, Richland, and the greater Tri-Cities region; andWHEREAS, During his service in the Senate, Senator Boehnke has served as Ranking Member of the Environment, Energy and Technology Committee and as a member of the Higher Education and Workforce Development, Ways and Means, and Rules committees, providing leadership on issues critical to Washington's economic future and technological advancement; andWHEREAS, Senator Boehnke has been a leading voice in strengthening Washington's technology and innovation economy, contributing to statewide and national conversations on cybersecurity, artificial intelligence, advanced data infrastructure, and policies that support responsible development of emerging technologies; andWHEREAS, Senator Boehnke has championed policies that expand career and technical education and workforce training opportunities, recognizing the importance of preparing students and workers for high-demand careers in cybersecurity, advanced manufacturing, energy, and other growing industries; andWHEREAS, Senator Boehnke has been a strong advocate for reliable and affordable energy, including support for advanced nuclear energy, clean energy innovation, and an electric grid capable of supporting Washington's agriculture, manufacturing, and growing technology sectors; andWHEREAS, Senator Boehnke has consistently championed the needs of the Tri-Cities, Eastern Washington, and rural communities, working to ensure their voices are heard in Olympia and promoting policies that strengthen agriculture, economic opportunity, and regional development; andWHEREAS, Senator Boehnke has earned the respect of colleagues across the aisle for his commitment to bipartisan problem solving, working collaboratively to pass legislation that improves student safety, expands access to higher education, strengthens mental health services, and supports workforce development; andWHEREAS, Prior to his legislative service, Senator Boehnke served more than 21 years in the United States Army aviation branch, retiring with distinction as a lieutenant colonel and demonstrating a lifelong commitment to leadership, service, and the defense of our nation; andWHEREAS, Outside the Legislature, Senator Boehnke has dedicated himself to education and workforce development as a tenured cybersecurity professor at Columbia Basin College, where he founded the college's cybersecurity program and has helped train the next generation of professionals to protect businesses, governments, and critical infrastructure from evolving digital threats; andWHEREAS, Senator Boehnke has encouraged civic involvement and mentored future leaders while representing his district with integrity, professionalism, and respect for the institution of the Legislature; andWHEREAS, Senator Boehnke's life and career reflect a deep commitment to faith, family, and community, and he has been supported throughout his years of public service by his wife, Dawn, to whom he has been married for more than 35 years, as well as their two sons and daughter-in-law;NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, That the Washington State Senate honor and commend Senator Matt Boehnke for his distinguished service, leadership, and dedication to the people of the 8th Legislative District and the State of Washington; andBE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That the Senate express its sincere gratitude for Senator Boehnke's contributions to innovation, workforce development, energy leadership, and steadfast advocacy for the Tri-Cities and the people of Eastern Washington; andBE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That copies of this resolution be immediately transmitted by the Secretary of the Senate to Senator Matt Boehnke and his family as a token of the Senate's respect and appreciation for his dedicated service to the State of Washington.I, Sarah Bannister, Secretary of the Senate,do hereby certify that this is a true andcorrect copy of Senate Resolution 8705,adopted by the SenateMarch 12, 2026SARAH BANNISTERSecretary of the Senate
