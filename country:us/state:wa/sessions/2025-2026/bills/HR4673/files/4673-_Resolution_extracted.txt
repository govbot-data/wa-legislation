ï»¿HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 2026-4673HR 4673ByRepresentative RuleWHEREAS, The Washington state sports community is celebrating prominence on the world stage as a site for the 2026 FIFA World Cup; andWHEREAS, More than half of youth in Washington state participate in organized athletics; andWHEREAS, Organized youth sports and physical activity can provide essential infrastructure for youth well-being with lifelong benefits; andWHEREAS, High quality youth sports support academic engagement and school attendance; promote resiliency and physical health; and protect against anxiety and depression; andWHEREAS, Only 24 percent of youth statewide meet the recommended 60 minutes of daily physical activity, with participation rates declining as children age; andWHEREAS, Washington state ranks 38th in youth well-being, according to Mental Health America; andWHEREAS, Disparities exist in access to organized sports, especially for girls, youth of color, children with disabilities, immigrants, children who reside in rural areas, LGBTQ+ youth, and children from low-income families, and with youth who do not speak English at home nearly three times more likely to never participate in sports; andWHEREAS, Young people thrive when their coaches are trained in youth development principles and when they have access to trained professionals who can assess injuries common to young athletes like concussions, heat strokes, stress fractures, knee injuries, and ankle sprains; andWHEREAS, Early specialization in youth athletics has been found to cause overuse injuries and burnout; andWHEREAS, Only approximately half of coaches have reported feeling prepared to support athletes with mental health concerns; andWHEREAS, Many communities lack sufficient fields and facilities to accommodate sports programming; andWHEREAS, Less traditional emerging sports such as ultimate frisbee, pickleball, flag football, roller derby, mountain biking, parkour, lacrosse, and martial arts provide valuable opportunities for youth development and should be recognized alongside mainstream sports; andWHEREAS, Adaptive sports are an opportunity for youth with disabilities to participate in and experience sports; andWHEREAS, All youth should be able to enjoy sports as a joyful and social experience;NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, That the Washington state House of Representatives recognize the transformative benefits of youth sports opportunities and celebrate inclusive, affordable, and high quality sports programs; andBE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That the state recognize the value of trained coaches and athletic staff to ensure all young athletes are safe and feel welcomed and supported; andBE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That the Washington state House of Representatives recognize nontraditional emerging sports as opportunities for more youth to be involved in athletics and celebrate youth sports initiatives as a means of supporting the well-being of youth in Washington state.I hereby certify this to be a true and correct copy ofResolution 4673 adopted by the House of RepresentativesJanuary 26, 2026__________________________Bernard Dean, Chief Clerk
