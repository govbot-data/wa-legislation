BySenators C. Wilson, Chapman, Cleveland, Dhingra, Hasegawa, Krishnadasan, Lovelett, Lovick, Nobles, Orwall, SaldaÃ±a, Shewmake, Slatter, and Trudeau
WHEREAS, Young people across Washington state represent the future of our communities, bringing creativity, resilience, and leadership to every corner of the state; and
WHEREAS, Scholars participating in the Boundless Washington, Compassion Scholars, and Washington World Fellows programs administered by the Washington State Leadership Board (WSLB) have demonstrated exceptional dedication to personal growth, leadership development, civic engagement, and community service; and
WHEREAS, The achievements of these young leaders exemplify the power of investing in education, leadership training, and inclusive opportunities, inspiring their peers and uplifting communities locally, statewide, and globally; and
WHEREAS, Since 2022, program participants have collectively earned more than $1.5 million in grants and scholarships for postsecondary education and activities, which will yield long-term benefits for the State of Washington by cultivating a highly skilled, innovative, and civically engaged workforce that strengthens the state's economic growth and competitiveness; and
WHEREAS, Through the support of their families and their own hard work, determination, and commitment to making a difference, these scholars embody the values of service, leadership, and perseverance, setting an example for generations to come;
NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, That the Senate of the State of Washington honor and celebrate the scholars who have participated in the Washington State Leadership Board programs for their extraordinary contributions, leadership, and dedication to shaping a brighter future for Washington state and beyond.
I, Sarah Bannister, Secretary of the Senate,
