ï»¿H-2724.1HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 4211State of Washington69th Legislature2026 Regular SessionByRepresentative DavisRead first time 01/13/26.Referred to Committee on Civil Rights & Judiciary.BE IT RESOLVED, BY THE SENATE AND HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE STATE OF WASHINGTON, IN LEGISLATIVE SESSION ASSEMBLED:THAT, At the next general election to be held in this state the secretary of state shall submit to the qualified voters of the state for their approval and ratification, or rejection, an amendment to Article IV of the Constitution of the state of Washington by adding a new section to read as follows:Article IV, section . . .. INTERBRANCH COMMISSION ON COURT RULES. (1) The supreme court shall have the power to promulgate rules of court to provide necessary governance of court procedure and practice. Court rules may not abridge, enlarge, or modify any substantive right, invade the prerogatives or encroach on the inherent powers of the legislative or executive branch of the government, or otherwise violate the separation of powers among the three coequal branches.(2) There shall be an interbranch commission on court rules, consisting of the following members, all of whom must be an attorney licensed to practice law in this state:(a) Three representatives of the legislative branch: The attorney for the speaker of the house of representatives, the senior leadership counsel for the second largest caucus of the house of representatives; and one person appointed by the president of the senate and confirmed by a two-thirds vote of the senate;(b) Three representatives of the executive branch: One person representing the governor's office, appointed by the governor; one person representing the attorney general's office, appointed by the attorney general; and one person representing local government, appointed by the governor in consultation with organizations representing the interests of cities and counties; and(c) Three representatives of the judicial branch: One court of appeals judge selected by a two-thirds vote of the court of appeals judges; one superior court judge selected by a two-thirds vote of the superior court judges; and one limited jurisdiction court judge selected by a two-thirds vote of the limited jurisdiction court judges.(3) Before adopting a court rule, the supreme court shall notify and forward to the interbranch commission on court rules the text of the proposed rule, along with all public comments that are submitted to the supreme court relating to the proposed rule. Within 90 days after receiving notification, the commission shall review the proposed rule and public comments and make a finding, by simple majority vote, as to whether there is sufficient evidence that the proposed rule may abridge, enlarge, or modify any substantive right, invade the prerogatives or encroach on the inherent powers of the legislative or executive branch of the government, or otherwise violate the separation of powers among the three coequal branches.(4) If the interbranch commission on court rules finds there is not sufficient evidence that the proposed rule may violate the separation of powers between the three coequal branches, the supreme court may proceed with adopting the proposed rule.(5) If the interbranch commission on court rules finds there is sufficient evidence that the proposed rule may violate the separation of powers between the three coequal branches, the supreme court shall appoint five justices pro tempore to hold a public hearing on the proposed rule and to issue a determination on whether the proposed rule abridges, enlarges, or modifies any substantive right, invades the prerogatives or encroaches on the inherent powers of the legislative or executive branches of the government, or otherwise violates the separation of powers among the three coequal branches. If the justices pro tempore so determine, the supreme court may not adopt the proposed rule.BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That the secretary of state shall cause notice of this constitutional amendment to be published at least four times during the four weeks next preceding the election in every legal newspaper in the state.--- END ---
