Read first time 02/14/26.Referred to Committee on Environment & Energy.
TO THE HONORABLE BOB FERGUSON, GOVERNOR OF THE STATE OF WASHINGTON:
We, your Memorialists, the Senate and House of Representatives of the state of Washington, in legislative session assembled, respectfully represent and petition as follows:
WHEREAS, The United States department of energy has issued a request for information inviting states to express interest in hosting nuclear lifecycle innovation campuses, a proposed new federal-state initiative that could modernize the nation's full nuclear fuel cycle and strengthen America's leadership in advanced nuclear energy; and
WHEREAS, As described in the department of energy's announcement, these proposed campuses could support activities across the full nuclear fuel lifecycle, including fuel fabrication, enrichment, reprocessing of used nuclear fuel, and disposition of waste, and could also encompass advanced reactor deployment, power generation, advanced manufacturing, and colocated data centers depending on state and regional priorities; and
WHEREAS, The establishment of innovation campuses is intended to support regional economic growth, attract private investment, encourage workforce development, catalyze technology leadership, and help build a coherent, end-to-end energy strategy to meet future power demands; and
WHEREAS, Nuclear energy is a nonemitting, reliable, and resilient source of baseload power that can contribute to energy security, grid stability, and the reduction of carbon emissions while supporting high-wage jobs in manufacturing, engineering, research, and operations; and
WHEREAS, Participation in the department of energy's request for information process by states will help ensure that local and regional prioritiesâsuch as workforce training, infrastructure readiness, economic diversification, and technology innovationâare fully considered in federal-state partnerships shaping the future of nuclear energy infrastructure; and
WHEREAS, Washington state has existing strengths in nuclear science and technology, a skilled energy workforce, and communities interested in expanding opportunities related to advanced energy industries;
NOW, THEREFORE, Your Memorialists respectfully request the Governor to:
(1) Express the state's interest in response to the United States department of energy's request for information on hosting one or more nuclear lifecycle innovation campuses and to communicate that interest to the secretary of energy and appropriate federal officials in a timely manner;
(2) Direct the appropriate state agency or interagency task force to prepare and submit a response to the department of energy's nuclear lifecycle innovation campuses request for information, articulating Washington's priorities, capabilities, and proposed scope of activities the state could host, including strategies for workforce development, infrastructure investment, technology leadership, and economic growth;
(3) Communicate to the secretary of energy the state's support for nuclear innovation and interest in partnering on a nuclear lifecycle innovation campus that aligns with Washington's economic, energy, and workforce goals; and
(4) Work with the state legislature, regional stakeholders, local governments, higher education institutions, and industry partners to develop a coordinated vision and plan that can maximize the economic and energy security benefits of participating in a federal-state nuclear innovation initiative.
